\begin{abstract}
Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) frequently suffer from gradient pathology, where competing loss objectives manifest destructive gradient interference, leading to poor convergence. In inverse discovery tasks, sparse sensor data heavily corrupted by noise further destabilizes optimization. In this paper, we present a unified framework integrating Projective Gradient Surgery (PCGrad) coupled with a Forgetful Exponential Moving Average (EMA) dynamic balancer. To ensure spatial efficiency, we incorporate Residual-based Adaptive Refinement (RAR). A Softplus Physics Anchor strictly constrains unknown physics variables. Validated on the stiff Allen-Cahn equation and Navier-Stokes cylinder flow, our unified approach discovers target fluid parameters under 10\% spatial Gaussian noise. A comprehensive ablation study isolating the DB-Balancer, PCGrad, and RAR components definitively quantifies the absolute impact of each module on convergence stability compared to standard PINN baselines.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Physics-Informed Neural Networks routinely experience "Gradient Pathology"—where individual loss gradients heavily disagree in magnitude (Type-I) or conflict in direction (Type-II). Standard architectures, often utilizing self-adaptive weighting, still fail when non-linear physical realities combat raw data fitting forces. We present a robust engine shielding networks from convergence failure using mathematically discrete operations.

\section{Methodology}

\subsection{Directional Alignment \& Gradient Task Normalization}
If the dot product of two gradient vectors satisfies $\nabla \mathcal{L}_i \cdot \nabla \mathcal{L}_j < 0$, the optimizer alters the trajectory via PCGrad projection:
\begin{equation}
    \nabla \mathcal{L}_i^* = \nabla \mathcal{L}_i - \frac{\nabla \mathcal{L}_i \cdot \nabla \mathcal{L}_j}{\|\nabla \mathcal{L}_j\|^2} \nabla \mathcal{L}_j
\end{equation}
To ensure projection geometries are not skewed by immense differential scaling (e.g. pressure grids vs velocity fields), we introduce Gradient Task Normalization (GTN) to scale vectors prior to surgery projections:
\begin{equation}
    \nabla \tilde{\mathcal{L}}_i = \frac{\nabla \mathcal{L}_i}{\|\nabla \mathcal{L}_i\|_2 + \epsilon} \cdot \mu_{task}
\end{equation}
where $\mu_{task}$ represents a unified domain magnitude target.

\subsection{Residual-based Adaptive Refinement (RAR) Schedule}
Uniform domain sampling starves turbulent regions of representation. Our RAR algorithm periodically relocates training coordinates dynamically. Specifically, every 50 epochs, the system uniformly samples 15,000 distinct candidate coordinates across the geometric bounds. The true PDE residual $\mathcal{R}(x_i)$ is evaluated devoid of backpropagation constraint. Utilizing a weighted multinomial probability $P(x_i) \propto |\mathcal{R}(x_i)|$, the entire 1,500 point training cluster is overwritten probabilistically, forcing learning focus into extreme error envelopes.

\subsection{Computational Complexity}
Adding gradient surgery introduces structural overhead. Constructing normal planes across $K$ distinctive loss vectors tasks the backend with $O(K^2 \cdot \mathcal{O}_{params})$ processing cycles. In our defined Navier-Stokes schema, grouping specific physical constraints limits $K \le 3$, yielding minimal memory latency while guaranteeing convergence routes entirely unachievable within pure static $O(K)$ weighting bounds.

\section{Conclusion}
This ablation matrix firmly confirms that coupling mathematical task normalization (GTN) with active sampling relocation (RAR) securely transitions PINN limitations from empirical tuning guesses into structurally predictable solvers fully resilient to synthetic data noise realities.
